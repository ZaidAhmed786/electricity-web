Hurricane Helene's Impact

Hurricane Helene has brought unprecedented damage, making it the most destructive storm our electricity system has ever faced.
Crews on the Ground Restoring Power

Our team has mobilized over 20,000 skilled personnel, doubling our initial workforce, to bring power back to those affected by the storm. We’re committed to working swiftly and safely.

Customers Still Affected

Approximately 200,000 customers remain without power, but our dedicated crews are working around the clock to ensure that every home and business is restored as soon as possible.

Progress So Far

We have already restored power to over 1.14 million customers, representing 85% of those impacted by Hurricane Helene. Our efforts continue until everyone is back online.

Please stay safe by avoiding downed power lines, trees, metal fences, and standing water, as they may be electrified.

Areas Still Without Power

Thank you for your patience as we work tirelessly to restore services.

Stay updated through our Outage Map, where you can find the latest restoration timelines. We’re doing everything we can to exceed these estimates and restore power as quickly as possible.

Despite ongoing damage and challenging conditions, our teams are on the ground 24/7, working until every customer is reconnected.

Although crews are facing obstacles like blocked roads, communication issues, and extensive tree damage, significant progress is being made in the hardest-hit areas, including Augusta, Savannah, Macon, and Valdosta.

Our restoration efforts prioritize public safety and essential services like hospitals and emergency facilities, followed by repairs that will restore power to the greatest number of customers in the shortest time.

Historic Damage to Georgia's Electric Infrastructure

As we respond to widespread outages from Hurricane Helene, our crews are navigating hazardous conditions, making immediate restoration difficult in some areas.

In several communities, the damage is so severe that we are essentially rebuilding parts of the grid, rather than performing simple repairs.

We’re inspired by the resilience of our communities and the tireless efforts of our crews and partners as we continue restoring power.

Our current efforts include:

  • Repairing or replacing over 8,000 power poles
  • Replacing 21,000+ spans of wire, stretching over 1,000 miles
  • Installing 1,500+ new transformers
  • Removing over 3,200 trees from power lines

How We Planned for Helene

Thanks to meticulous planning, technology, and preparation, we were able to respond swiftly to outages and restore power as soon as conditions allowed.

Our preparation included:
  • Monitoring Hurricane Helene’s path over a week in advance.
  • Pre-positioning crews, equipment, and supplies near areas likely to be affected, including additional transformers, poles, and cables.
  • Engaging with our mutual assistance network, bringing in teams from over 35 companies across multiple states.
  • Collaborating with partners like Alabama Power and Mississippi Power, forming the largest response force we've ever mobilized, even surpassing previous hurricanes like Michael and Irma.
Technology played a crucial role:
  • Smart grid technology helped us identify and resolve issues faster, rerouting power where needed.
  • Drones and helicopters allowed for faster damage assessment and quicker dispatch of crews.
